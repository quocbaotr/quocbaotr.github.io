Interactive Web Project Research on Strike.Amophic.Space

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing that caught my attention was the central animated logo on the webpage. Its dynamic movement and vibrant colors immediately drew my focus.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Clicked on the interactive logo to trigger animations.
  • Scrolled down to explore different sections.
  • Hovered over various shapes and elements to see if they were interactive.
  • Clicked on the navigation links to move between pages.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the interactive "Color Shape" section. The ability to change colors and shapes by clicking and dragging was intriguing and kept me engaged.

4. What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was clicking and hovering to trigger animations and interactions. I clicked on different elements to observe their responses.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

My impression is that the primary goal of the interactive experience is to engage users in creative exploration and experimentation with interactive shapes, colors, and animations.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The interactive experience communicates its primary goal through the use of interactive elements and animations that respond to user input. The "Color Shape" section, in particular, encourages users to manipulate and play with shapes and colors.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The experience can be interacted with for short bursts of creative exploration, lasting a few minutes at a time. Users may return multiple times to experiment with different shapes and color combinations.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The experience's intuitive design and easily discoverable interactive elements communicate that it's meant to be explored over time. The dynamic animations and ability to change shapes and colors suggest ongoing engagement.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience references forms of generative art, interactive multimedia installations, and abstract animations often seen in digital art exhibitions.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The references communicate that I should approach the experience with a sense of creativity and experimentation. I'm encouraged to interact and manipulate elements to discover various visual outcomes.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The references communicate that I should feel curious and intrigued while engaging with the experience. It suggests that I can expect to encounter dynamic visual surprises during my exploration.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part was the occasional difficulty in accurately clicking and dragging shapes to adjust their sizes. This was frustrating because it disrupted the fluidity of my interactions.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part was successfully creating harmonious color and shape combinations that transformed into visually pleasing animations. The immediate response to my interactions was gratifying.
